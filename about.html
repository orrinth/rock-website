Rock Formation

Rocks are made starting with lava, or magma, which is basically lava within the earth. Notice that when lava is hot, it glows with a white hot color, but when the lava cools, its glow fades until dim. The lava cooling starts the solidification process into a new rock.

Once the lava cools completely, the glow fades completely, and its solidification into a rock finishes. Soon, a new rock is made, usually in the form of igenous. However, it's important to note that this process is a cycle. For more information about this cycle, visit the Facts page.
